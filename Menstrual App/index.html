Remember: consent, protection, and accurate information matter. Visit local health services for confidential advice.

Safe Sex

  • Use condoms consistently to prevent STIs and unwanted pregnancies.
  • Open communication with partners about sexual history and consent.
  • Regular STI testing.
  • Practice mutual respect and boundaries.

Contraceptives

  • Barrier methods: condoms, diaphragms.
  • Hormonal methods: pills, patches, injections.
  • Long-acting reversible contraception: IUDs, implants.
  • Effectiveness varies; consult a healthcare provider.

Surgical Ways to Avoid Pregnancy & After Effects

  • Permanent methods: tubal ligation for females, vasectomy for males.
  • Highly effective but irreversible.
  • Possible side effects: minor pain, swelling, infection risk.
  • Consult a qualified doctor before decision.

Pills

  • Oral contraceptives prevent ovulation and reduce pregnancy risk.
  • Must be taken consistently at the same time daily.
  • May have side effects: nausea, headaches, mood changes.
  • Consult a doctor before starting pills.
